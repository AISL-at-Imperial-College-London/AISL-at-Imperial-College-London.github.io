Autonomous Industrial Systems Lab

Welcome to our Website, here we are advancing research of autonomous systems in chemical engineering.

Our Mission

Industrial processes today are highly automated but remain dependent on human operators for supervision and decision-making. At AISL, we develop adaptive, trustworthy, and explainable autonomous systems that augment human expertise and enable safer, more efficient industrial operations.

Why Autonomous Systems Matter

Workforce Challenges

Demographics and employment shifts are creating operator shortages that threaten production of essential goods and economic growth.

Technology Access

Advanced control and optimization remain expensive and inaccessible to small and medium enterprises seeking improved efficiency.

Safety & Reliability

Automation can reduce human errors and improve consistency in decision-making, preventing costly failures and accidents.


Research Areas

Automation

Advanced automation systems for industrial applications and autonomous control.

Machine Learning

AI and machine learning techniques for autonomous decision-making.

Control Systems

Novel control strategies for complex autonomous systems.

Industrial Decarbonization

addressing energy management challenges in electrified systems, renewable energy integration, and complex multi-generation heating and cooling networks.
